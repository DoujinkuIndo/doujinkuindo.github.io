At this year’s KubeCon + CloudNativeCon EU 2021, some things were the same — it was still virtual which meant it attracted a huge turnout of a more broadly international audience — and some things were different — like that almost everyone’s bought into Kubernetes and cloud native architecture, it’s now just how they use it. In another KubeCon tradition, The New Stack hosted a live pancake breakfast to reflect on the maturity of Kubernetes particularly around data persistence and storage.

The New Stack publisher Alex Williams hosted this year’s discussion with Itzik Reich, Dell Technologies vice president, and Nivas Iyer, Dell senior principal product manager, along with pancakes regular Cheryl Hung, vice president of ecosystem at the Cloud Native Computing Foundation.
